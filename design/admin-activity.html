Nobody writes these entries by hand. The feed is produced by a decorator around the service layer: any create, update, delete or state transition that passes through a service is recorded with its actor, entity and before/after values, so a new endpoint is audited the day it ships without anyone remembering to add a log line. The same table backs the "Recent" section of the ⌘K palette — recent items are simply this actor's last few touched entities, which is why the two can never drift apart.
