Terms of service

These terms govern the use of this website and the engineering work HaizoTech carries out for clients. Where a signed agreement or statement of work says something different, that document takes priority over this page.

Last updated: 19 July 2026

1. Who these terms are between

“HaizoTech”, “we” and “us” mean HaizoTech, a software engineering studio based in Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu, India. “You” and “the client” mean the business engaging us for work, or anyone using this website.

2. Scope of services

We design, build, deploy and support custom software: applications, databases, APIs, integrations and the infrastructure they run on. What we will actually deliver on a given engagement is set out in the statement of work, not here.

Anything not written into the statement of work is out of scope. That's not a technicality — it's how we keep estimates honest. If you want something added, we'll price it and agree it in writing as a change before we build it.

The content on this website, including case studies and service descriptions, is general information. It isn't an offer, a warranty, or professional advice for your specific situation.

3. Quotes and starting an engagement

  • Quotes are based on the requirements you give us. If those requirements turn out to be materially different, we'll tell you as soon as we know and re-quote rather than quietly absorb it or quietly bill it.
  • A quote is valid for 30 days from the date we issue it, unless it says otherwise.
  • An engagement begins when you accept the statement of work in writing and, where one is required, the initial payment is received.
  • We schedule work against agreed dates. Timelines assume you provide access, approvals, content and decisions when they're needed; where they're delayed, delivery dates move accordingly.

4. Payment

  • Fees, currency, taxes and the payment schedule are set out in the statement of work.
  • Unless agreed otherwise, invoices are payable within 15 days of the invoice date.
  • Project work is normally billed in milestones. Retainers and support are billed on a recurring cycle, in advance.
  • Third-party costs we incur on your behalf — hosting, domains, licences, paid APIs — are passed through at cost and either billed to you or paid from accounts in your name. We'll always tell you before committing you to a recurring cost.
  • If an invoice is significantly overdue, we may pause work after giving you written notice. We'll resume once it's settled. Interest may apply to late payment at the rate stated in the statement of work.

5. Intellectual property — you own it

This section matters more than any other on the page, because it's the core of how we work: you own what we build for you.

  • On full payment for the relevant milestone, all rights in the bespoke source code, database schemas, data and design deliverables created for you under the statement of work transfer to you. That includes the copyright.
  • No vendor lock-in. We build on open-source technologies. You receive the repository, the deployment configuration and the documentation, and you are free to run, modify, extend, or move the system — with us, with another team, or in-house.
  • We do not keep a licence you have to renew in order to use your own system, and we do not withhold anything as leverage.
  • Pre-existing material. We may reuse general-purpose components, libraries, tooling and know-how we owned before the engagement or developed independently of it. These stay ours, and we grant you a perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free licence to use them as part of the delivered system. We won't build your system so that it depends on anything we can revoke.
  • Third-party components. Open-source and third-party dependencies stay under their own licences. We'll tell you which ones the system uses.
  • Your material. Your content, data, trade marks and existing systems remain entirely yours. You grant us only the access we need to do the work.
  • Publicity. We will not name you, publish a case study, or use your logo without your written permission.

6. Confidentiality

Each side will keep the other's confidential information confidential, use it only for the engagement, and share it only with people who need it and are under equivalent obligations. This covers your business data, roadmaps, credentials and commercial terms.

It doesn't cover information that is already public through no fault of the receiving party, was already lawfully known, is independently developed, or must be disclosed by law — and in that last case we'll tell you first, if we're legally allowed to.

These obligations continue after the engagement ends. Where you have a separate NDA with us, that agreement governs.

7. Warranties and their limits

  • We warrant that we'll perform the work with reasonable skill and care, to professional industry standards, and that we have the right to grant the rights described above.
  • Defect warranty. For 90 days after a deliverable is accepted, we'll fix defects — deliverables not working as the statement of work describes — at no charge. New requirements and changes of mind aren't defects; they're changes, and they're quoted.
  • The warranty doesn't cover problems caused by changes made by you or a third party, misuse, failures of third-party services, or environments we don't control.
  • We don't warrant that software will be uninterrupted or error-free. No one honestly can. Beyond the warranties written here, and to the extent the law allows, all other warranties and conditions are excluded.

8. Liability

Neither party excludes liability for anything that can't lawfully be excluded — including fraud, or death or personal injury caused by negligence.

Subject to that: neither party is liable for indirect or consequential loss, loss of profit, loss of revenue, loss of business, or loss of anticipated savings. Each party's total aggregate liability arising out of an engagement is limited to the total fees paid under the relevant statement of work in the 12 months before the claim.

You are responsible for maintaining your own backups of your data, and for the accuracy and legality of the data and content you provide to us.

9. Termination

  • Either party may end an engagement for convenience by giving 30 days' written notice.
  • Either party may end it immediately if the other commits a material breach and hasn't fixed it within 30 days of being told in writing, or becomes insolvent.
  • On termination you pay for work completed and costs properly committed up to that point. Once that's settled, we hand over the code, credentials and documentation for everything paid for — a termination is not a reason to withhold your system.
  • The sections on intellectual property, confidentiality, liability and governing law survive termination.

10. Acceptable use of this website

Don't attempt to breach, probe or disrupt this site, don't scrape it at a volume that degrades it for others, and don't submit unlawful content through our forms. We may block access where any of that happens.

11. Personal data

How we handle personal data is set out in our privacy policy. Where we process personal data on your behalf as part of an engagement, we do so on your documented instructions under a separate data-processing agreement.

12. Changes to these terms

We may update these terms for future engagements and website use. The version that applies to an active engagement is the one in force when the statement of work was signed. Changes are published on this page with an updated date.

13. Governing law and jurisdiction

These terms and any engagement under them are governed by the laws of India. The courts at Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu have exclusive jurisdiction over any dispute, and both parties submit to that jurisdiction.

Before starting proceedings, both parties agree to try to resolve the dispute in good faith by talking to each other first.

14. General

  • If any part of these terms is found unenforceable, the rest continues to apply.
  • Neither party may assign an engagement without the other's written consent, except as part of a sale of substantially the whole business.
  • Nothing here creates a partnership, joint venture, or employment relationship between us.
  • Neither party is liable for failures caused by events genuinely beyond its reasonable control.
