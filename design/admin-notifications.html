A single summary email instead of one per event. Security notifications are never bundled into it.

Preferences control delivery, not the record. Every notification is written to the database whether or not it is ever delivered, so this screen stays a complete history even for someone who has switched everything off — and so a missed handover can be reconstructed after the fact. Two categories deliberately ignore preferences: password resets and invites always send an email, because those are the messages that tell someone their account is being changed, and letting a setting suppress them would turn a preference into a security hole.
